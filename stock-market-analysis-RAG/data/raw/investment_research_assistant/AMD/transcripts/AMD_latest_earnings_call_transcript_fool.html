Summary

Advanced Micro Devices (AMD +0.04%) delivered record financial performance with 34% revenue growth and notable expansion across data center, client, and AI business lines, driven by strong EPYC and Instinct adoption. Product launches and customer engagements accelerated, resulting in substantial share gains and historic levels of cloud and enterprise deployments. Nonrecurring China GPU sales contributed $390 million to quarterly results, with gross margin strength boosted by a $306 million inventory reserve release, while management limited forward guidance on China due to regulatory uncertainty. Cash flow and buybacks reached all-time highs, and management guided for continued aggressive investment in AI, with data center revenue growth possibly exceeding 60% in 2026 as MI450 and Helios platform ramps begin in the second half.

  • Management confirmed that Q1 2026 revenue includes only $100 million of China GPU sales with no further forecast due to a very dynamic situation.
  • "Customer pull for Venice is very high," and management expects broad OEM platform launches when the next-generation Venice CPU becomes available later in the year.
  • Data center CPU and GPU demand are modeled to rise throughout 2026, with supply chain and multiyear capacity planning cited to support anticipated growth.
  • The MI450 series will ship in RackScale solutions, with the vast majority of it is going to be RackScale solutions of 2026 revenue recognized on shipment to rack builders, per Lisa Su.
  • Operating leverage is expected as revenue scales, with OpEx growth anticipated to trail revenue expansion, particularly in the second half with the AI ramp.
  • Embedded segment achieved record design win momentum, supported by $17 billion in design wins in 2025, up nearly 20%
  • Management emphasized the importance of gross margin mix, with MI355 and Turin ramps contributing positively and further gross margin improvement forecast as MI450 scales.
  • Gaming segment faces an expected significant double-digit percentage revenue decline in 2026 from mature console cycle dynamics; next-gen Xbox ramping is cited for 2027.

Industry glossary

  • EPYC: AMDâ€™s family of high-performance server CPUs designed for data centers and cloud infrastructure.
  • Instinct: AMDâ€™s suite of data center GPU accelerators targeting AI and high-performance computing workloads.
  • MI350/MI355/MI400/MI450/MI455x/MI430x/MI440x/MI500: Successive AMD Instinct GPU series, with varying focus on mainstream AI, supercomputing, sovereign AI, and enterprise inference/training.
  • Venice/Turin/Genoa: Code-names for different generations of AMD EPYC server CPUs.
  • RackScale: Data center system architecture that delivers GPU or compute capacity in rack-integrated solutions, allowing rapid large-scale deployment.
  • ROCm: AMDâ€™s open software platform for GPU computing and AI model deployment.
  • OEM: Original Equipment Manufacturerâ€”companies that integrate AMD chips into their computing systems.
  • Semi-Custom SoC: Application-specific System-on-Chip solutions co-designed with major hardware brands (e.g., for gaming consoles).
  • HBM: High Bandwidth Memoryâ€”cutting-edge memory technology integrated into advanced GPUs for AI and HPC.
  • AIPC: AI-enabled Personal Computers equipped with dedicated AI hardware or capabilities.
